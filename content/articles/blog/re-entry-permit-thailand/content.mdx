This article is for people who:

- nearly lost an extension after a holiday,
- manage staff or family members with Thailand extensions.

## Do you need a re-entry permit?

**You need a re-entry permit before leaving Thailand when departure would otherwise cancel your current extension or long-stay permission.** If your status is single-entry, exiting without re-entry typically ends the extension tied to that entry.

**You may not need one when** your permission is already multiple-entry and survives departure under current rules, or when you intentionally end the stay and will apply for a new category abroad.

**When in doubt, confirm at immigration before you fly.** Losing a year-long retirement or business extension because of a weekend trip is a common and expensive mistake.

## Single vs multiple re-entry permit

**Single re-entry** allows you to leave once and return while preserving your underlying permission, subject to validity dates.

**Multiple re-entry** allows repeated exits and returns during the validity period. It suits residents who travel regionally for work or family.

| Type | Best for | Risk if you skip it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Single re-entry | One planned trip during your stay period | Extension cancelled on departure |
| Multiple re-entry | Frequent travel before permission ends | Same cancellation risk on every trip |

Neither type extends your permission beyond the underlying end date unless you receive a separate extension.

**Apply at Thai immigration before departure**, usually at the office that handles your province's extensions. Practice varies slightly by location, but the normal file includes your passport, application form, photos, and fee per current office rules.

## What happens if you leave without one

**Your extension or long-stay permission is usually cancelled when you depart on single-entry status without valid re-entry.** When you return, you may receive only a short tourist stamp unless you qualify for a new long-stay category.

**Recovery is expensive:** new embassy applications, lost extension time, employer disruption, and school enrollment complications. Retirement and business residents who travel often should treat re-entry as **standard pre-flight compliance**, not an optional extra.

## Decision framework

**Apply for re-entry before travel when:**

- Immigration confirms your status is single-entry
- You hold an extension you must preserve
- You will return before your permission end date
- You travel frequently during the same permission period (consider multiple re-entry)

**You may skip re-entry when:**

- You intentionally end the stay and will apply for a new visa abroad
- Immigration confirms your permission already survives departure
- You are on short tourist entry without an extension to protect

**Get case review when:**

- Stamps are unclear or damaged
- You recently changed visa category
- You overstay even one day before applying
- An employer or school deadline depends on your return date

## Common mistakes

### Assuming "extension" automatically means multiple-entry

Extensions do not always change entry type. **Read the stamp language**, do not guess from the word extension.

### Applying at the airport on departure day

Immigration offices, not airline counters, issue re-entry permits. **File early** with buffer for office queues.

### Confusing re-entry with a new visa

Re-entry preserves existing permission. It does **not** replace [Change Visa Type in Thailand](/blog/change-visa-type-in-thailand) when you need a new category.

### Returning after permission end date

Re-entry requires you to return within valid dates. **Calendar permission end date and flight dates** together.

### Ignoring re-entry after retirement or business extensions

Long-stay residents in every category travel. Retirement applicants should pair this article with [Retirement Visa O vs O-A Thailand](/blog/retirement-visa-o-vs-o-a-thailand) planning, not skip compliance.
