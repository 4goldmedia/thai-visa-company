## Why do DTV applications get rejected?

**Most DTV rejections are evidence and intent problems, not random embassy decisions.** Officers must believe you meet Destination Thailand Visa rules and that your documents are authentic, consistent, and complete.

**The five refusal families:**

1. **Financial evidence** that does not meet balance, seasoning, or letter format rules
2. **Activity and intent** that does not support remote work or qualifying freelance patterns
3. **Document quality** issues such as missing pages, wrong visa photos, or unsigned forms
4. **Travel and compliance history** that raises overstay or prior refusal concerns
5. **Processing post variance** where stricter interpretation exposes borderline files

Embassy refusal letters are often brief. **Do not wait for a full explanation** before you audit your file.

## Financial evidence rejection reasons

**Financial refusals mean the officer did not accept your proof of funds or income stability**, even when you believed you met the headline number.

### Bank balance below effective threshold

DTV rules publish minimum balances, but **embassy practice may expect margin** above the minimum, especially if your account was recently funded.

### Seasoning and sudden deposits

Large transfers shortly before application trigger **source-of-funds questions**. Officers want to see stable balances, not last-minute top-ups without explanation.

### Bank letter format errors

Letters that omit required wording, stamp types, account holder names, or currency clarity are **rejected as invalid evidence**, not as minor corrections.

### Income proof that does not match DTV activity

Payslips from a Thai employer, local business revenue, or pension-only income **may not support DTV remote-work intent**. The financial story must match the category.

### Mixed currencies and scattered accounts

Multiple small accounts without a clear primary proof account can **weaken credibility**. Consolidate and document deliberately before filing.

## Intent and activity rejection reasons

**Intent refusals mean your stated purpose does not align with DTV**, or your supporting activity proof is thin.

### Unclear remote-work narrative

You claim digital nomad status but provide **no contracts, client letters, portfolio, or tax evidence** showing ongoing foreign work.

### Thai employment signals on a DTV file

Job offers, Thai company invoices, or WP3 documents on a DTV application suggest **wrong category**. Employment belongs on [Business Visa vs Work Permit](/blog/business-visa-vs-work-permit-thailand).

### Retiree story on a DTV application

Applicants 50+ sometimes file DTV with pension-only finances. That is usually a **category mismatch**, not a document typo. Compare [DTV vs Retirement Visa](/blog/dtv-vs-retirement-visa-thailand) before you reapply.

### Passive income without qualifying activity pattern

Rental dividends alone may not match current DTV activity expectations. **Read the visa page activity definitions** rather than assuming any foreign income qualifies.

### Education or local business plans

If you will study or operate a Thailand-facing business, **DTV is likely the wrong intent**. Compare [DTV Visa vs Education Visa Thailand](/blog/dtv-vs-education-visa-thailand) or use the [Thailand Business Visa](/visas/business) page instead.

## Document and admin rejection reasons

**Admin refusals are preventable** with checklist discipline.

### Incomplete e-Visa uploads

Missing pages, blurry scans, or wrong file order cause **technical refusals** without reaching merits review.

### Passport validity and condition

Passports too close to expiry, damaged data pages, or **insufficient blank pages** can stop processing early.

### Wrong jurisdiction or nationality post

Applying at an embassy without jurisdiction over your nationality or residence **wastes time** and may yield refusal.

### Inconsistent personal data

Name spelling differences across passport, bank letter, and employment letters create **integrity doubts**.

### Prior refusal not addressed

Reapplying without fixing the first refusal pattern **signals poor preparation**.

## Decision framework

**Stay on DTV and reapply when:**

- Refusal likely traced to fixable document formatting or seasoning
- Your remote-work intent is genuine and you can prove activity
- You corrected bank letters with the issuing branch
- You are not secretly planning Thai employment

**Pivot away from DTV when:**

- Your income is primarily pension or passive retirement funds
- You accepted a Thai job and need Non-Immigrant B
- Repeated refusals show structural mismatch, not typos
- Tourist entry was your backup plan (compare [DTV vs Tourist Visa](/blog/dtv-visa-vs-tourist-visa-thailand))

**Get case review before reapplication when:**

- You received multiple refusals
- Large recent deposits funded your balance
- You mix freelance, employment, and investment income
- An agent filed without showing you the final upload package

## Common mistakes

### Reapplying the same bank letter with a new date

Officers compare patterns across attempts. **Rebuild proof**, do not refresh dates only.

### Embassy shopping without fixes

Changing posts while keeping weak intent evidence **rarely works** and can harm credibility.

### Entering Thailand on tourist to "wait out" a refusal

Tourist status does not repair a DTV file. It creates **overstay risk** if you stay hoping for in-country conversion. See [Change Visa Type in Thailand](/blog/change-visa-type-in-thailand).

### Buying Elite to avoid fixing DTV evidence

Elite is expensive and not automatic approval. Read [Thailand Elite Visa Worth It](/blog/thailand-elite-visa-worth-it) only after an honest DTV gap analysis.

### Hiding prior refusals from your agent

Agents cannot fix what they do not see. **Disclose full history** so your next category choice is lawful.
