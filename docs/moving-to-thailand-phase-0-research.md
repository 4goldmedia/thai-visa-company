# Moving to Thailand — Phase 0 Editorial Research

**Purpose:** Identify real search intent before any further copy or structural changes.  
**Status:** Research complete — no page changes made.  
**Date:** June 2026

---

## Executive summary

People researching a move to Thailand follow a predictable journey: emotional pull → feasibility check → practical logistics → legal route → first-year settling → long-term planning. The highest-value questions cluster around **whether Thailand fits their life** (not which visa first), **what daily life actually costs**, **where to live**, **how to work legally**, and **what will frustrate them in year one**.

The current page already maps well to core intent themes after the recent intent-based restructure. The largest gaps are **taxes and financial compliance**, **housing/renting depth**, **immigration admin (90-day reporting, TM30, re-entry)**, **healthcare insurance specifics**, **relocation logistics**, and **profile-specific visa edges** (marriage, spouse, trial visits). Visa content should remain at the end of the journey — but several compliance topics belong in the life guide, not only on visa hubs.

## 3. Search-intent analysis

### Journey stages

```
Awareness → Evaluation → Planning → Legal route → Relocation → Settling → Long-term
```

| Stage | User mindset | Top questions | Page priority |
|-------|--------------|---------------|---------------|
| Awareness | "Thailand looks appealing" | Why move? Is it good? Pros/cons? | Sections 1–3 |
| Evaluation | "Could this work for me?" | Cost? Cities? Everyday life? Families/retirement/work? | Sections 4–10 |
| Planning | "How do I actually do this?" | Housing, banking, healthcare, challenges | Sections 8–11 + gaps |
| Legal route | "What visa do I need?" | Which visa? Work legally? | Section 12 (end) |
| Settling | "First 90 days" | Bank account, lease, TM30, 90-day report | Mostly gaps today |
| Long-term | "Year 2+" | Taxes, PR, re-entry, visa renewals | Gaps + blog links |

### Intent vs. current SEO landscape

Competitors and forums repeatedly surface topics our page underweights:

1. **Tax residency (180-day rule)** — frequently searched post-2024 rule changes  
2. **Housing mechanics** — deposits, agent fees, lease terms, condo vs house  
3. **Immigration admin** — 90-day reporting, TM30, re-entry permits  
4. **Insurance for older residents** — age limits, pre-existing conditions  
5. **Trial relocation** — tourist entry → long-stay conversion pattern  
6. **Marriage / spouse visas** — common forum topic; no site visa hub (link only if content exists)

### AEO citation requirements

For AI systems to cite a section confidently, it needs:

1. A clear question as the H2  
2. A direct 1–2 sentence answer in the first paragraph  
3. Specific, non-vague facts (ranges, conditions, trade-offs)  
4. Standalone completeness (no "see above")

**Current page strength:** Most sections already use question titles + `quickAnswer`.  
**Weakness:** Several subsections are thin (1 paragraph); housing, taxes, and compliance lack dedicated answers.

## 6. Recommendations (no implementation yet)

### A. Keep the current intent-based section order

The flow matches the research journey. Do not revert to utility sections (standalone weather, banking, etc.) unless they answer a top-20 question on their own.

### B. Priority content additions (Phase 1 writing)

**High impact — add before cosmetic redesign:**

1. **Housing intent** — Either expand challenges + FAQ or add a dedicated "How do I find a place to live?" block covering search process, deposits, lease length, condo vs house, trial-stay strategy.  
2. **Taxes & financial compliance** — FAQ + short subsection under challenges or new "Long-term living" theme: 180-day residency, foreign income remittance (high-level, not tax advice).  
3. **Immigration admin** — 90-day reporting, re-entry permits, change visa: FAQ entries + links to existing blogs.  
4. **Healthcare insurance depth** — Expand retirement + families + FAQ: private vs insurance, age considerations, hospital choice.  
5. **International school costs** — Expand families + cost section with realistic range language.  
6. **Trial relocation pattern** — Explicit answer: visit on exemption → validate city → apply long-stay visa.

**Medium impact:**

7. Bangkok vs Chiang Mai comparison paragraph in cities intro  
8. Hidden / startup costs in cost of living  
9. Driving license + road safety expansion in challenges  
10. Digital nomad cluster link from working section  

**Supporting (FAQ only):**

11. Pets, medications, shipping belongings  
12. Marriage visa (no hub — honest "depends on circumstances, seek advice")  
13. LTR visa (no site hub — do not create placeholder)

### D. AEO formatting standards (for Phase 1 writing)

Every major section must follow:

```
## [Exact user question]?

[1–2 sentence direct answer]

[2–3 paragraphs of practical context]

### [Sub-question if needed]
[Short answer → detail]

**Related:** [natural links only to existing pages]
```

### E. Internal linking rules (unchanged)

- Link only to existing visa pages and published blog articles  
- No placeholder URLs  
- Visa links appear after life questions are answered  
- Decision hub: `best-visa-for-living-in-thailand`  
- Lifestyle hub: `can-foreigners-live-in-thailand-permanently`

### F. Success metrics for Phase 1

After content work, the page should:

- Answer all **High** intent questions (items 1–8, 11–18, 26–29, 35–36, 41, 43, 46–50, 56–57, 59, 61–63, 64) with a dedicated answer
